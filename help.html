InspectEquip Help

InspectEquip™ is designed to help workshops, mechanics, and operators record and generate inspection, breakdown, and repair reports quickly and efficiently.

This page covers common questions about inspections, breakdowns, repairs, customers, vehicles, subscriptions, device transfers, reports, tutorials, and troubleshooting.

Getting Started

From the home screen, you can access inspections, breakdown reports, repair reports, customer and vehicle management, subscription management, and settings.

To begin an inspection, tap Inspections, start a new inspection, select the vehicle or equipment type, enter the job details, and complete the checklist items as required.

Each inspection is automatically assigned a unique Inspection ID and records inspection results, notes, photos, and report information.

Item Condition

  • OK — Item appears serviceable or operating normally at the time of inspection.
  • ADVISORY — Item may require monitoring, maintenance, or attention soon, but may not currently require immediate repair.
  • DEFECT — Fault, damage, wear, or condition identified that may require urgent repair, replacement, or further assessment.
  • N/A — Item was not applicable, not fitted, or not inspected as part of the inspection.

Inspection items may also be left unselected if they are not required as part of the report. Unselected items are hidden from generated reports by default. This behaviour can be enabled or disabled in Report Settings.

Breakdown Reports

Breakdown reports are used to document faults, findings, repairs carried out, parts used, machine status, and related notes when a vehicle, machine, or piece of equipment has broken down.

To create a breakdown report, tap Breakdowns from the home screen and create a new report. Enter the customer, vehicle or machine details, then complete the breakdown details and status fields.

Breakdown reports can include photos, signatures, technician comments, status information, and a breakdown-specific disclaimer based on your Report Settings.

Repair Reports

Repair reports are used to document repair or rectification work completed on a vehicle, machine, or piece of equipment.

To create a repair report, tap Repairs from the home screen and create a new report. Enter the customer, vehicle or machine details, then add one or more repair items.

Each repair item can include the fault or defect reported, technician findings, repair work carried out, parts used, and photos.

Repair reports can include photos, signatures, technician comments, status information, and a repair-specific disclaimer based on your Report Settings.

Saving Progress

Inspection, breakdown, and repair progress is stored locally while you work. However, data is currently stored locally on your device only.

Important:
If your device is lost, reset, damaged, or the app is removed, locally stored inspections, breakdowns, repairs, customers, vehicles, and reports may be permanently lost.

Generating Reports

Inspections, breakdowns, and repairs can generate PDF reports containing details such as:

  • job information
  • customer details
  • vehicle, machine, or equipment details
  • inspection results, breakdown details, or repair items
  • notes and technician comments
  • photos
  • signatures
  • status information
  • report disclaimers

Reports can be printed, shared, emailed, or saved to your device or cloud storage.

We recommend using a cloud storage service such as Google Drive, iCloud Drive, OneDrive, or Dropbox if you want additional backup protection for your generated PDF reports.

Customers and Vehicles

Customer and vehicle records are stored locally on your device and can be used when creating inspections, breakdown reports, and repair reports.

From the Manage Customers page, you can add, edit, search, and delete customer records and their associated vehicles.

Exporting and Importing Customers and Vehicles

InspectEquip can export and import customer and vehicle data to help with backups, moving to a new phone or tablet, or setting up an additional workshop device.

From the Manage Customers page, use the menu in the top right to export or import customer and vehicle data.

The export creates a ZIP file containing customer and vehicle data only.

The export does not include:

  • inspections
  • breakdown reports
  • repair reports
  • PDF files
  • photos
  • inspection history or report history
  • subscriptions
  • licence keys or device access details

When importing, customers and vehicles are merged into your existing data. Existing data is not wiped. Duplicates are skipped where possible.

Important:
Customer and vehicle export files may contain business or customer information. Only share export files with people or devices you trust, and delete old export files when they are no longer needed.

Report Settings and Disclaimers

Report Settings allow you to manage your company header, contact details, logo, report display options, and report disclaimers.

Inspections, breakdown reports, and repair reports can each have their own disclaimer text and disclaimer visibility setting.

You can edit or reset each disclaimer from Report Settings.

Subscriptions & Licence Access

InspectEquip uses a licence-based subscription system rather than user accounts.

Access to subscription features depends on:

  • a valid subscription
  • device registration
  • subscription status
  • periodic entitlement verification

Some subscription plans may allow multiple registered devices.

Restore Purchases

Use Restore Purchases if you already have an active subscription and need to restore access on another device using the same app store account.

Important:
Purchases can only be restored within the same platform ecosystem:
  • Android purchases can only be restored on Android devices using the same Google Play account.
  • Apple purchases can only be restored on iPhone or iPad using the same Apple ID.
Cross-platform restoration is not supported.

For example:

  • Android → Android = supported
  • iPhone → iPad = supported
  • Android → iPhone = not supported
  • iPhone → Android = not supported

If changing platforms, you may need to cancel the existing subscription through the original app store and subscribe again on the new platform.

Moving to a New Device

If your subscription device limit has already been reached, InspectEquip may require recovery email verification before transferring access to a new device.

Once verified:

  • the new device may become the active manager device;
  • older devices may lose subscription access; and
  • subscription management may move to the newly verified device.

Customer and vehicle data is not automatically transferred as part of subscription access recovery. Use the customer and vehicle export/import feature if you need to move customer and vehicle records to another device.

Recovery Email

A recovery email can be used to help recover subscription access and manage registered devices.

You should ensure your recovery email remains accessible and up to date.

Losing access to your recovery email may prevent you from recovering access to your subscription on a new device.

Offline Access

InspectEquip supports temporary offline access after successful subscription verification.

The app may periodically require an internet connection to:

  • verify subscription status
  • maintain subscription access
  • restore purchases
  • manage devices

If entitlement verification cannot be completed within the allowed period, access to subscription features may become restricted until verification succeeds again.

PIN Security

PIN security is enabled by default to help protect data stored on the device.

Changing your PIN from Settings will not erase your stored data.

Resetting a forgotten PIN may permanently erase locally stored inspections, breakdowns, repairs, customers, and vehicles.

Troubleshooting

Restore Purchases does not find my subscription

Make sure:

  • the subscription is still active;
  • you are signed into the correct Google Play or Apple account;
  • you are restoring on the same platform the subscription was purchased on; and
  • the device has an active internet connection.

My subscription transferred to another device

Some subscription plans limit the number of registered devices. Recovering access on another device may replace the previously active device.

My reports, customers, or vehicles are missing

Inspection, breakdown, repair, customer, and vehicle data is currently stored locally on the device. Deleted app data, device resets, app removal, or device loss may result in permanent data loss.

If you previously exported customer and vehicle data, you can import the ZIP file from the Manage Customers page.
