InspectEquip Help

InspectEquip™ is designed to help workshops, mechanics, and operators record and generate inspection reports quickly and efficiently.

This page covers common questions about inspections, subscriptions, device transfers, reports, and troubleshooting.

Getting Started

To begin an inspection, open the app and tap Start New Inspection. Select the inspection type, enter the job details, and complete the checklist items as required.

Each inspection is automatically assigned a unique Inspection ID and records inspection results, notes, photos, and report information.

Item Condition

  • OK — Item appears serviceable or operating normally at the time of inspection.
  • ADVISORY — Item may require monitoring, maintenance, or attention soon, but may not currently require immediate repair.
  • DEFECT — Fault, damage, wear, or condition identified that may require urgent repair, replacement, or further assessment.
  • N/A — Item was not applicable, not fitted, or not inspected as part of the inspection.

Inspection items may also be left unselected if they are not required as part of the inspection. Unselected items are hidden from generated reports by default. This behaviour can be enabled or disabled in Report Settings.

Saving Progress

Inspection progress is saved while you work. However, inspections are currently stored locally on your device only.

Important:
If your device is lost, reset, damaged, or the app is removed, locally stored inspections may be permanently lost.

Generating Reports

Once an inspection is completed, you can generate a PDF report containing:

  • Job information
  • Inspection results
  • Notes
  • Photos
  • Inspection summary

Reports can be printed, shared, emailed, or saved to your device or cloud storage.

Subscriptions & Licence Access

InspectEquip uses a licence-based subscription system rather than user accounts.

Access to subscription features depends on:

  • a valid subscription
  • device registration
  • subscription status
  • periodic entitlement verification

Some subscription plans may allow multiple registered devices.

Restore Purchases

Use Restore Purchases if you already have an active subscription and need to restore access on another device using the same app store account.

Important:
Purchases can only be restored within the same platform ecosystem:
  • Android purchases can only be restored on Android devices using the same Google Play account.
  • Apple purchases can only be restored on iPhone or iPad using the same Apple ID.
Cross-platform restoration is not supported.

For example:

  • Android → Android = supported
  • iPhone → iPad = supported
  • Android → iPhone = not supported
  • iPhone → Android = not supported

If changing platforms, you may need to cancel the existing subscription through the original app store and subscribe again on the new platform.

Moving to a New Device

If your subscription device limit has already been reached, InspectEquip may require recovery email verification before transferring access to a new device.

Once verified:

  • the new device may become the active manager device;
  • older devices may lose subscription access; and
  • subscription management may move to the newly verified device.

Recovery Email

A recovery email can be used to help recover subscription access and manage registered devices.

You should ensure your recovery email remains accessible and up to date.

Losing access to your recovery email may prevent you from recovering access to your subscription on a new device.

Offline Access

InspectEquip supports temporary offline access after successful subscription verification.

The app may periodically require an internet connection to:

  • verify subscription status
  • maintain subscription access
  • restore purchases
  • manage devices

If entitlement verification cannot be completed within the allowed period, access to subscription features may become restricted until verification succeeds again.

PIN Security

PIN security is enabled by default to help protect inspection data stored on the device.

Resetting a forgotten PIN may permanently erase locally stored inspection history.

Troubleshooting

Restore Purchases does not find my subscription

Make sure:

  • the subscription is still active;
  • you are signed into the correct Google Play or Apple account;
  • you are restoring on the same platform the subscription was purchased on; and
  • the device has an active internet connection.

My subscription transferred to another device

Some subscription plans limit the number of registered devices. Recovering access on another device may replace the previously active device.

My reports or inspections are missing

Inspection data is currently stored locally on the device. Deleted app data, device resets, or device loss may result in permanent data loss.
